This file documents any notable observations made from the simulations themselves, from
physical observations and to analysis of the code's behavior through the simulation.
NOTE: The word "iteration" here commonly refers to a loop in logic, for example: One iteration is the 
same as one relaxation loop in the analysis on relative error below.

Relative error vs number of relaxation loops show an overall trend down between runs when
the max relaxation loop limit is reached. We see that the relative error increases slightly
when the cirlces move, indicating that it is a good measure of the difference in potential field
from where it is to where it should be. Some frames show a VERY large increase in the relative
error, this corresponds to when the electric field around a circle increases greatly, leading to
a circle moving over a significant distance, thus leading to another large change.
